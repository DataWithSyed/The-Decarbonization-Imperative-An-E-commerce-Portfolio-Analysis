The Baseline: Where We Stand

Our analysis begins with a three-year historical view of the carbon footprint for our portfolio of three diverse e-commerce brands. The data reveals a clear upward trend, with a combined portfolio emissions growth rate of 12.28% annually. This trajectory sets the stage for our forward-looking analysis.

The Fork in the Road: Two Futures

Using the historical growth rate, we've projected emissions over the next ten years. This presents two starkly different paths: a "Business as Usual" scenario, where emissions grow unchecked, and a "Decarbonization Target" scenario, where we implement an aggressive but achievable 7% annual reduction. The Blueprint for Action

Achieving the Decarbonization Target requires a focused, multi-pronged strategy. Our recommendations center on three high-impact areas that address the primary drivers of emissions in the e-commerce value chain.

Material Decarbonization

The highest impact lies in changing what our products are made of. We must prioritize sourcing materials with a certified lower footprint, such as shifting from virgin to recycled polyester, which can cut material emissions by over 70%.

Logistics Optimization

We need to tackle emissions from packaging and delivery. This means reducing packaging weight, eliminating plastics, shifting from air to sea freight, and partnering with last-mile carriers that utilize electric vehicle fleets.

Supplier Engagement

True decarbonization requires systemic change. We must move beyond industry averages by collecting primary data from our key suppliers and collaborating with them to transition their manufacturing facilities to 100% renewable energy.
